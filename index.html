Green Lines

The green lines represent the varsity swim teams trips to their fall international dual meet.
Last year, the University of Toronto travelled to Las Vegas, Nevada to compete against the Unviersity of Nevada. This past fall, the team travelled to New York City where the men competed against Columbia University and the women competed against Rutgers University.
These trips are a sepcial opportunity for the team to experience university swimming in a different country and compete against new swimmers.

Orange Lines

The orange lines represent the teams trips to their week long training camps.
In the 2022-23 season, the team flew to Barbados for a little more than a week on New Year's day. This year, the team travelled to Fort-Lauderdale, Florida during the fall reading week.
Training camps are crucial periods of training where swimmers can focus on training hard. But they are also crucial times of team bonding in the sun and at the beach.

Purple Lines

The purple lines represent the teams trip to the OUA Championship meets.
These meets are hosted by universities within Ontario, as the OUA Championship meet is the Ontario Provincial University Championship meet. The University of Toronto hosted the OUA Championship meet in 2022, whereas Western University hosted it 2023 and Brock Univeristy will host it this year.
The University of Toronto's womens team has won the OUA Championship every year since 2014 while the mens team has won every year since 2004.

Blue Lines

The dark blue lines represent where I traveled to, or am going to travel to, to compete at the USports Championship swim meet, as part of the varsity swim team at the University of Toronto.
Last year, Usports were in Victoria, BC, hosted by the University of Victoria. And the year before that they were at Laval University in Quebec City. This year they are hosted by McGill University in Pointe-Claire, Montreal.
Usports Championships are the biggest swim meet of our season. It's where every university in Canada competes for the National title. University of Toronto has won the past two years, and we are hoping to win it for a third time in a couple of weeks.
